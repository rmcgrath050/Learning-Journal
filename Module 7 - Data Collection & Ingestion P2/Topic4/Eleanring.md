# L5DE 7.4 - Monitoring an ingestion service and anomaly detection techniques

Without proper monitoring, these pipelines can become bottlenecks, leading to data loss, performance issues, and compliance risks. For example

Important for: 
- Ensure data integrity: Detect and prevent data loss or corruption.
- Maintain performance: Identify bottlenecks and optimise resource utilisation.
- Enhance reliability: Quickly detect and respond to system failures or anomalies.
- Support compliance: Meet regulatory requirements for data handling and reporting.


###### Key Concepts for monioring:
- metrics (Quantitative measures of system performance (e.g., throughput, latency))
- Logs, Records of events and messages generated by applications and systems.
- Alerts , Notifications triggered by specific conditions or thresholds.
- Dashboards, Visual representations of metrics and system status.


In the world of data engineering, having the right tools to monitor and visualise your data pipelines is crucial. Two of the most powerful and widely-used tools in this domain are Prometheus and Grafana. These open-source platforms have become industry standards for their robust features and flexibility.
