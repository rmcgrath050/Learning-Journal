# Recap of data quality fundamentals

No organisation has perfect business processes, technical processes, or data management practices. As a result, all organisations experience problems related to the quality of their data. Those that formally manage data quality have fewer problems than those that leave it to chance

### Accuracy

Accuracy refers to the degree that the data correctly represents real-life entities. Accuracy is difficult to measure unless an organisation can reproduce data collection or manually confirm the accuracy of records. Most measures of accuracy rely on comparison to a data source that has been verified as accurate, such as a system of record or data from a reliable source.

### Completeness

Completeness refers to whether all required data is present. Completeness can be measured at the data set, record, or column level. Some example questions to judge completeness are:

- Does the data set contain all the records expected?
- Are records populated correctly? Records with different statuses may have different expectations for completeness.
- Are column attributes populated to the level expected? Some columns are mandatory. Optional columns are populated only under specific conditions.
